# Concept Brief: Zavari
*Revised 2026-06-25 · Skill: 3-concept v2.0*

> Concept screens for the Phase 3 gate. Pushed to "Zavari — Phase 3 Concepts" (Project 2), anchored to the Zavari Design System (Project 1). These screens **sell the vision** and **prove the wedge**; the buildable MVP is highlighted *inside* the larger picture, never shown alone.

---

## Frame: sell intelligence, not insurance

Lead with the **proactive, generative** value — Zavari surfaces opportunities and decision-ready intelligence you didn't think to ask for. Coherence and second-order reasoning are part of *how the intelligence is uniquely good*, **not** a defensive alarm system. Offense, not defense; a multiplier, not a safety net. Every surface should feel like *"Zavari made me see something I couldn't,"* never *"Zavari caught my mistake."*

---

## What these screens must demonstrate

Three things, without captions:

1. **Proactive intelligence (the lead).** Zavari *proactively* generates the strategic intelligence that matters — it surfaces opportunities, drafts decision-ready briefs, and flags what you didn't think to ask, continuously and between planning cycles, not only when queried. Because it triangulates across the whole stack, that intelligence is uniquely good: it reasons about **second-order consequences** and catches where strategy is **incoherent** (bets that conflict or don't ladder to the north star) — all traceable. The proactivity and the cross-ecosystem reach are what no single-ecosystem incumbent can match.
2. **The vision.** Zavari is an *ecosystem*: that intelligence layer sits atop a stack of lightweight, owned first-party tools (plus the tools a company already uses), and the intelligence lives in the **connections between them**.
3. **First-party-led entry.** You **start with Zavari's own tools** (foot-in-the-door, useful standalone, natively wired to the intelligence) and **expand by connecting your stack** when ready. The owned stack is the *foundation*, not a secondary on-ramp.

And the buildable **MVP wedge** must be legible *inside* the vision — a viewer should see both where this goes and what ships first.

---

## The ecosystem the screens depict

Model it as **tools (nodes) + bridges (links) + the intelligence layer**, tagged by who owns each.

- **Owned first-party tools — the "capable middle"** (lighter than Confluence / Jira / Jira Align, more structured than Docs / Sheets): **Strategy/OKR · Planning/Roadmap · Research (qual + survey-quant) · Quant (thin product-metric capture) · Doc.** The bit of canonical structure that makes each tool feel light to the user is the same structure that makes it legible to the engine.
- **Bridges — where coherence is computed:** **ROI / investment-weighing** (Strategy ↔ Planning) · **Continuous feedback / VoC** (Research ↔ Planning) · plus the engine's own cross-links. A bridge is literally "does this initiative ladder to that objective / does this feature trace to a real insight."
- **AI-native or BYO:** **Market & Industry Intelligence** — synthesized from the open web, or imported (Gartner / Nielsen). Differentiated by *packaging*, not raw synthesis.
- **BYO integrations — the expansion layer:** Analytics · Delivery (Jira / Linear) · Docs (Confluence / Notion) · *(deferred: CRM, Data Warehouse)*.
- **Owned outputs (the intelligence layer):** Strategic Command Layer · Strategic Opportunity Briefs · Coherence map · Decision Record.
- **Near-term owned focus (what the MVP is drawn from):** Strategy · Planning · Research · Quant + their two bridges.

**The secret sauce to make visible:** raw AI synthesis is a commodity; Zavari's edge is the *framework + presentation* that packages the proactive intelligence — the Brief, the coherence map, the finding / confidence / provenance treatment. Invest the most design care there.

## Screen inventory (~9–11 screens; you decide the exact breakdown)

Intent-driven — for each, the **outcome/feeling** matters more than a component list. Add, split, or merge where it tells the story better. The order leads with the proactive intelligence payoff, then reveals how deep it goes.

### Act 1 — Proactive intelligence (lead with the payoff)
**I1 — Strategic Command Layer (home).** Frames the home around **what Zavari surfaced for you** since you last looked — proactively generated opportunities, a fresh brief, a signal that matters — alongside the active bets it bears on and the sources feeding it (owned + AI-native + BYO as peers).
*Must achieve:* the first feeling is *"it's already been working for me"* — generative, not a status board; instant trust; Zavari sits *above* the stack.

**I2 — Strategic Opportunity Brief.** The marquee output — a decision-ready brief Zavari **generated proactively**, with second-order findings and coherence read-outs, each sourced and confidence-rated, leading to a recommendation.
*Must achieve:* something a leader would stake a decision on; the *packaging* is unmistakably differentiated — intelligence, not a report.

### Act 2 — Why the intelligence is uniquely good (the proof)
**I3 — Coherence map (marquee differentiator).** Active bets against the north star, surfacing conflicts / non-laddering — the **bridges visualized** — framed as *insight* ("here's what we see across your whole stack"), calm, never an alarm.
*Must achieve:* the clearest proof of the cross-ecosystem wedge; misalignment legible at a glance as *intelligence*, not a warning light.

**I4 — Decision Record / strategy-to-execution.** The owned, auditable reasoning chain, with provenance forward into BYO delivery (a Jira epic carrying its "why").
*Must achieve:* the unbroken chain feels trustworthy and effortless to follow.

### Act 3 — The platform (the vision)
**V1 — The ecosystem map ("the platform").** The whole picture on one surface: the intelligence layer atop the owned tools, the bridges between them, AI-native market signal, and BYO expansion — visually distinguishing **owned / AI-native / BYO**, with the **near-term owned set and the MVP wedge highlighted**.
*Must achieve:* having felt the payoff, the viewer now sees how deep it goes — an operating system for strategy — *and* the buildable starting point, in one glance.

### Act 4 — The owned stack (foundation / where you start)
**O1 — Strategy/OKR.** The owned strategic surface — north star, objectives, the canonical strategic format.
*Must achieve:* lighter and more coherent than Jira Align; structure that invites, never bureaucratic.

**O2 — Planning/Roadmap.** Initiatives / backlog / specs — the sheet↔Jira middle — with the **ROI-weighing bridge** to Strategy visible (this initiative laddered to that objective).
*Must achieve:* a small team would happily start here; the link to strategy is felt, not buried.

**O3 — Research.** Qual insights + survey-quant, with the **Feedback/VoC bridge** to Planning.
*Must achieve:* effortless capture; the path from a customer insight to a prioritized bet is visible.

*(Quant — thin product-metric capture — may appear as a panel within O2/O3 or its own light view; your call.)*

### Act 5 — Entry
**E1 — Two-pronged, first-party-led onboarding.** "**Start with our tools**" foregrounded (owned, useful standalone, pre-wired to intelligence); "**connect your stack**" as the expansion path.
*Must achieve:* the owned path feels like the natural, low-commitment start; BYO reads as "turn on more when ready," not the main event.

## Design anchors
- **Design system:** Zavari Design System (Project 1) — established brand, IBM Plex, Navy/Cyan/Azure. Lean on `SourceTag` (provenance; owned sources = cyan-dot, first-class peers), `CoherenceFlag`, and the Brief layout.
- **Implementation target:** Tailwind (Phase 6) — keep structure Tailwind-friendly.
- **Register:** authoritative yet genuinely intuitive; the owned tools must feel **capable-middle** (light for the user, structured underneath).
- **Value framing:** **intelligence, not insurance** — proactive and generative throughout; coherence shown as insight, never alarm.
- **Differentiation lives in presentation** — invest the most design care in the proactive Briefs / coherence / findings.
- **Primary user:** VP Product / CPO / Head of Strategy at a growth-stage product org. Desktop-first, high-focus.
- **Fidelity:** real-product feel — not pixel-final, not a wireframe.
- **Always highlight the MVP wedge** within the vision wherever the ecosystem is shown.

---

## Scope
**These are:** concept screens for a commit-or-kill gate — high enough fidelity to feel like a real product and let the founder judge the concept.
**Navigable:** the screens must be **easy to traverse as a connected set** — the concept should be walkable end-to-end, not a folder of isolated mockups. This will likely manifest as functional navigation (a persistent nav, a screen index, or inter-screen links), but the implementation is Claude Design's call.
**These are not:** production designs, pixel-final, or a complete product. No settings / account / auth plumbing — design the product, not the plumbing. And **showing the vision ≠ implying it's all built** — keep the buildable MVP clearly legible inside it.
