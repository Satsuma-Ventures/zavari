# Thesis Document: Zavari (WIP)

*2026-06-24 · Author: Satsuma Ventures · Skill: 2-thesis v1.6*

---

**The Landscape**

Product organizations run on siloed tools — research (Dovetail), analytics (Amplitude), market intel (Crayon), roadmap (Productboard/JPD), delivery (Jira/Linear), docs (Notion/Confluence) — each now bolting on its own AI synthesis. Four ecosystems (Atlassian, Miro, Amplitude, Microsoft) are extending intelligence *within their own data gravity*, and AI is driving a broad **re-bundling** toward trusted platforms over best-of-breed stacks (Tunguz, Mehta). The incumbent coherence layer is now real, not theoretical: Atlassian's **Strategy Collection / Strategic Intelligence in Focus** entered open beta in **June 2026**, reasoning over its 150-billion-connection Teamwork Graph to tell executives what's on track, at risk, and needs a decision — which validates the category as much as it competes in it.

**The Gap**

No **vendor-neutral** layer triangulates across the qual + quant + market + execution signals a company actually uses *regardless of which ecosystem owns them*, and surfaces cross-team strategic incoherence at the right altitude. Incumbents detect coherence only inside their own graph; the company whose signal is scattered across Dovetail + Amplitude + Figma + Notion + the open web has no in-ecosystem answer. It is unbuilt because the value requires breadth of integration before it appears (cold-start) and depends on access to data the ecosystem owners control.

**The Two-Pronged Entry**

Zavari resolves the cold-start with two complementary on-ramps to the same coherence layer: **(1) BYO — connect the tools you already use** (vendor-neutral integration), and **(2) Use ours — aggressively-priced lightweight first-party tools** (research, roadmap, planning) that own canonical data models and reduce dependence on competitor APIs. Either path seeds the intelligence layer; the pitch is "use our tools for less, and turn on the strategic intelligence when you're ready."

**Market Opportunity** *(directional — estimates, not projections; founder considers these conservative)*

- **TAM ~$10–12B** — combined product management, OKR/strategy execution, competitive intelligence, and AI analytics categories.
- **SAM ~$180–420M+** — growth-stage product orgs at a $30–60K platform ACV (ICP stage held open). Likely understated: the first-party-tools prong extends the reachable market into the qualitative-research category alone (~$577M in 2025 → ~$1.2B by 2032, ~11% CAGR) and adjacent point-tool markets, plus a longer ICP runway as the platform grows with a company.
- **SOM (Yr 1–3): low single-digit millions ARR (deliberately conservative).** Primarily execution-bound rather than demand-bound.

**Net Positive Impact**

Weak on its own terms — primary benefit accrues to well-resourced companies deciding better, with less wasted engineering effort as the main second-order effect; no obvious societal externality. This is the **most material open question for Satsuma fit** (see Risk 3). To be answered genuinely, not shoehorned, before any Track 2 commitment.

**What We Believe**

- We believe the durable value is **cross-ecosystem strategic coherence** — detecting where initiatives conflict or don't ladder to the north star, across the tools a company already uses — *not* signal synthesis (commoditizing) or forecasting (unbuildable and untrusted).
- Because AI is re-bundling software toward trusted platforms, buyers are consolidating (8–12 tools → 2–4), and **no incumbent can be vendor-neutral** — their coherence stops at the edge of their own graph.
- Which means a vendor-neutral coherence layer, entered via both BYO integration and lightweight first-party tools, can land standalone and expand into the intelligence platform.
- And we are positioned to act because of deep practitioner domain insight and AI-native build economics — agentic tooling (e.g., Lovable-class builders) now make lightweight first-party tools and integrations buildable by a small team, turning a play that historically required a large org into one a solo founder can *validate* and then staff and raise behind.

*Falsifiable:* if buyers prefer in-ecosystem coherence (Atlassian, Amplitude) to vendor-neutral, or if neither entry prong pulls through to the platform, the thesis is wrong.

**Why Now**

AI re-bundling + buyer consolidation + AI-collapsed build cost make a compound play viable for a small team for the first time, while a major incumbent (Atlassian) is actively educating the market on strategic intelligence — heavy lifting a nimble, differentiated entrant can ride. The same forces mean the window is **months, not years**.

**Right to Win**

*Real:* genuine founder domain expertise in this exact problem; AI-native execution speed (Satsuma's core advantage) materially lowers the build cost that historically gated a compound/ecosystem play, making solo-stage validation realistic. *Honestly contested:* platform dependency on competitor-controlled APIs; no distribution against incumbents who own it; and the solo, non-technical starting profile — a validation-stage reality to be resolved with a team and a raise once there is "enough smoke," not a permanent condition.

**Top Three Risks**

1. **Differentiation durability — getting bundled away.** Atlassian educating the market is net-helpful, but the real threat is that the vendor-neutral wedge isn't defensible enough and incumbents/bundlers absorb it. — *Mitigation:* own canonical data via the first-party prong, hold the cross-ecosystem neutrality incumbents structurally can't, and move fast while the window is open.
2. **Platform dependency / co-opetition.** The integration prong depends on API access from vendors who are also competitors and can throttle or revoke it (Twitter/X, Facebook precedent). — *Mitigation:* the first-party lightweight-tools prong — a real and growing pattern in enterprise software — reduces reliance on any single vendor's API.
3. **Net positive impact / Satsuma fit.** The thesis is weak on Satsuma's "value created beyond the customer" filter — a genuine gate criterion, and the most material fit question. — *Mitigation:* identify a credible, non-shoehorned net-positive angle (e.g., reducing organizational waste and dysfunction; democratizing strategic rigor for under-resourced teams) and validate it before the Track 1 terminal commit.

**Recommendation**

**Proceed to Phase 3 — Concept.** The gap is specific and defensible, the two-pronged entry credibly addresses the cold-start, and AI-native build economics make solo-stage validation realistic. This Phase 2 gate is a small step (concept the product, not commit to building it). The harder questions — team/co-founder + raise, and a genuine net-positive-impact answer — belong to the Track 1 terminal (Stage 1→2) gate, where the adversarial-review bar applies.
