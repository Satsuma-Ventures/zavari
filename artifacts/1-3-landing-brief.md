## Purpose

A single-page public landing that does three jobs, in order:
1. **Sell the vision** — Zavari is proactive strategic intelligence that surfaces what you didn't think to ask, across your whole stack.
2. **Make the wedge legible** — a sophisticated visitor (CPO / VP Product) sees both where this goes *and* what they can start with today.
3. **Convert to the right next step** — a design-partner / early-access conversation, not a self-serve signup.

This is the front door of the public Track 1 repo. It sits alongside the concept screens (`docs/concepts/zavari-concepts.html`) and the brand page (`docs/brand.html`) but is the page a stranger lands on first.

## ⚠️ Framing note — use the *current* concept direction, not the older positioning doc

`context/zavari-positioning-messaging.md` (March 2026) is rich and authoritative on **voice, audience, and proof points** — mine it for those. Its dominant frame ("strategic intelligence **OS**," "**Triangulation Engine**," "synthesis layer," coherence-as-safeguard) predates the Phase 3 concept direction. The landing **blends the two**: it *leads* with the reconciled Phase 3 v2.0 direction, but keeps the **"strategic intelligence OS" category line at real prominence** — it's the sharpest one-liner for this buyer.

**Lead with (the emotional/experiential frame):**
- **Intelligence, not insurance.** Proactive and generative throughout. Every surface should feel like *"Zavari made me see something I couldn't,"* never *"Zavari caught my mistake."* Coherence is shown as **insight**, never as an alarm or a safety net.
- **First-party-led entry is co-equal, foregrounded.** "**Start with Zavari's own tools**" (lightweight, useful standalone, pre-wired to the intelligence) is the natural low-commitment start; "**connect your stack**" is the expansion path — not the headline.
- **Offense, not defense; a multiplier, not a safety net.**

**Keep prominent (the category frame):**
- The **"strategic intelligence OS for product organizations"** category line earns a featured spot — a hero category line or a dedicated category band. It's the crispest way a CPO understands *what this is*. The OS metaphor = "it runs beneath your stack and coordinates it," not literal infrastructure. Don't let it tip the page into a defensive/governance read, and don't let it crowd out the proactive lead — pair them (the proactive payoff is *what it does for you*; the OS line is *what category it's in*).

Keep the rest of the named-capability language (Triangulation Engine, Strategic Opportunity Brief, Decision Record, Strategic Instinct) as *supporting* texture. Where the docs genuinely conflict on tone (e.g. coherence-as-safeguard vs coherence-as-insight), **this brief wins.**

## Venture context (for the page's own voice)

Zavari is proactive strategic intelligence for senior product leaders (CPO, VP Product, Head of Strategy at growth-stage / Series B–D product orgs). It continuously surfaces decision-ready opportunities and briefs you didn't think to ask for — reasoning across qualitative research, product/behavioral signal, and market intelligence, with every conclusion traceable to its sources. You can **start with Zavari's own lightweight tools** (strategy, planning, research, quant) and **connect the rest of your stack when ready** — the intelligence lives in the connections between them.

The buyer is sophisticated and allergic to hype. The page earns trust through **precision and restraint**, not superlatives.

---

## Emotional register

Authoritative · proactive · precise · calm-confident · peer-to-peer · genuinely intuitive. It should read like an instrument built by a CPO for CPOs — and feel like it has *already been working for you*.

## What it must NOT feel like

- A "dashboard / analytics tool" landing (chart-salad hero, metric-grid bragging).
- A defensive / governance / "catch your mistakes" tool — no alarm-red, no warning-light motifs, no compliance energy.
- Generic AI-SaaS hype ("revolutionary," "transformative," gradient-blob hero, robot/brain imagery).
- An efficiency / time-savings pitch. The value is **better decisions**, not hours saved.

## Reference points (aesthetic adjacency, not copying)

- **Linear** — restraint, precision, confident typographic hierarchy, product-shots-as-proof.
- **Stripe (early product pages)** — authoritative, technical credibility without coldness, generous structure.
- **The Zavari concept screens themselves** (`docs/concepts/`) — the landing should feel like the same product, same world. Pull real product surfaces (the Strategic Opportunity Brief, the coherence map, the command layer) into the page as the hero/section visuals rather than inventing new abstractions.

## Section inventory (CareerChief structure, adapted)

Mirror CareerChief's page rhythm (`Nav · Hero · ProofStrip · HowItWorks · ScoreCard · HonestSection · Testimonial · FooterCTA`), re-tuned for Zavari. Add/merge where it tells the story better.

### Nav
Logo (use the provided light-bg lockup; never redraw/recolor), a couple of anchor links (How it works · The product · Early access), one primary CTA ("Request early access" / "Talk to us").

### Hero
Lead with the proactive payoff, and seat the OS category line right beside it:
- **Category line (prominent, e.g. eyebrow/kicker or sub-headline):** *"The strategic intelligence OS for product organizations."* This is the crisp "what is it" — give it featured placement, but never as the sole headline.
- **Headline:** the proactive payoff — *intelligence that surfaces what you didn't think to ask*, across your whole stack.
- **Subhead:** names the two-pronged reality (start with our tools, connect your stack).
- One primary CTA + one secondary ("See the concept" → links to `concepts/zavari-concepts.html`).
- Visual: a real Strategic Command Layer / Brief surface from the concept set, not an abstract illustration.
*Candidate payoff line (from positioning, on-frame):* "The signal nobody thought to ask for — surfaced before it becomes a problem." Founder/Claude Design may sharpen.

*(Optional category band just below the hero: the one-line OS framing expanded — "runs beneath your existing stack and coordinates it" — if the hero alone can't carry both the payoff and the category without crowding.)*

### ProofStrip
A restrained band of credibility. Use **sourced** numbers only, attributed correctly:
- Founder outcomes (usable now): **125% margin growth**, **35% CLV increase**, **3× faster planning cycles** (Virtuoso, AI agent workflows).
- The gap stat, attributed as *"Miro-commissioned Forrester/HBR research, September 2025"*: **87% say effective product development is critical; only 34% say they're effective.** (Never present as neutral third-party data.)
Keep it sparse and authoritative — not a logo wall of fake customers.

### HowItWorks — the ecosystem in three beats
Show the model: **owned first-party tools (nodes) → bridges where coherence is computed → the proactive intelligence layer on top.** Distinguish **owned / AI-native / BYO** visually (same language as the concept's ecosystem map). Make the **MVP wedge legible inside the vision** (Strategy · Planning · Research · Quant + their two bridges) — show where this goes *and* what ships first. Three steps or a single annotated ecosystem visual, Claude Design's call.

### ScoreCard → "The marquee output"
CareerChief's ScoreCard analog = Zavari's **Strategic Opportunity Brief** (or coherence map). Show one decision-ready, proactively-generated brief with second-order findings and source/confidence treatment. This is the page's "money shot" — invest the most design care here. Differentiation lives in the **packaging** (finding / confidence / provenance), not raw synthesis.

### HonestSection
Required, per Zavari voice ("honest about what doesn't exist yet"). State plainly: design-partner program is active; proof points from customer decisions are being earned; this is early. Sophisticated buyers trust the page *more* for it. Calm and confident, not apologetic.

### Testimonial
Founder credibility as the stand-in until design-partner quotes exist — the founder's practitioner hook (led product orgs to 125% margin growth / 35% CLV; built this synthesis manually for years; Zavari is the institutional version). Frame as practitioner POV, not a customer quote we don't have. Leave a clearly-marked placeholder for a real design-partner quote.

### FooterCTA — two-pronged, first-party-led
The conversion moment, mirroring the product's entry model:
- **Primary:** "Start with Zavari's tools" / "Request early access" (low-commitment, the foregrounded path).
- **Secondary:** "Connect your stack" / "Talk to us about your stack" (expansion path).
Plus logo, minimal footer links (brand page, concept). The owned path should feel like the natural start; BYO reads as "turn on more when ready."

## Design anchors

- **Design system:** Zavari Design System (Project 1) — established brand: IBM Plex, Navy / Cyan / Azure. Honor locked brand assets (logos as-is; palette + semantic roles). Source tokens from `design-system/colors_and_type.css` and `DESIGN.md`; reuse `SourceTag` (owned sources = cyan-dot, first-class peers), `CoherenceFlag`, Brief layout, `StatCallout` for the proof numbers.
- **Stat figures = Serif** (editorial), per the Phase 3 reconciliation — overrides the brand guide's Mono for stat display, deliberately.
- **Viewport:** Desktop-first, fully responsive to mobile. Visitors arrive on laptops in a high-focus context, but the page must hold up shared on a phone.
- **Voice:** practitioner, not vendor. Precise over promotional. Outcomes over features. Multiplier over efficiency. (See positioning doc "Tone and Voice.")

## docs/ deployment conventions (must follow exactly)

The page is served via GitHub Pages from the repo **`docs/` root** as `index.html`. So:
- **Self-contained or co-located.** Either a single self-contained `index.html`, or `index.html` plus assets/components under `docs/` with **relative paths** that resolve from `docs/`. No references to files outside what gets synced to `docs/`.
- **CDN fonts only** (Google Fonts `@import`/`<link>` for IBM Plex). No local font files. (Avoid the inlined-base64-font bloat that pushed the concept bundle past 1.7 MB — a landing page should stay light.)
- **Working internal links** to sibling pages already in `docs/`: `concepts/zavari-concepts.html` and `brand.html`.
- **Favicon** using the provided Zavari icon SVG.
- No analytics, no third-party trackers, no auth — this is a public concept-stage landing.

## What this is NOT

Not a product app, not a pricing page, not a self-serve signup, not a full site. One page, one job: make a sophisticated product leader want the early-access conversation, and trust that the product behind it is real.
