# Concept Brief: Zavari
*2026-06-24 · Skill: 3-concept v2.0*

> The concept screens Claude Design generates after the design system is complete. Pushed to the "Zavari — Phase 3 Concepts" project as `CONCEPT_BRIEF.md`, anchored to the Zavari Design System project.

---

## What these screens need to demonstrate

That Zavari is the **vendor-neutral layer above the stack** that does what no single-ecosystem incumbent can — triangulate signal across *all* of a company's tools and surface **strategic incoherence** (bets that conflict or don't ladder to the north star) plus the **second-order consequences** of a decision, with every conclusion traceable to its sources — **and** that Zavari meets a company two genuinely co-equal ways: **connect what you already use**, or **adopt Zavari's own lightweight tools** for research, roadmap, and planning.

Someone moving through these screens should understand, without captions: (1) Zavari sits *above* a vendor-neutral stack, with two real on-ramps; (2) its first-party point tools are light, inviting, and a legitimate reason to choose Zavari — not a backseat add-on; (3) its marquee output is a defensible, source-traceable answer; (4) it detects coherence conflicts an in-ecosystem tool structurally cannot see. The differentiation must be *visible and intuitive*, not technical.

---

## Two screen groups

The concept spans two connected experiences. The complexity of Zavari's value justifies the larger set — roughly **9–11 screens total**. The groupings and intentions below are the guide; **decide the exact screen breakdown yourself** — split, merge, or add screens where it tells the story better. Don't treat any list as exhaustive, and don't omit something the story needs because it wasn't named.

For each screen the brief gives **what it shows**, **why it earns a place**, and **what it must achieve** (the outcome/feeling — not a component spec). Design whatever components deliver those outcomes.

## Group A — The Strategic Intelligence Layer

*The differentiated coherence layer. The reason Zavari is defensible.*

### A1 — Strategic Command Layer (home)
**Shows:** The product home for a VP Product / CPO — active strategic bets with health (on track / drifting / at risk), the connected vendor-neutral stack feeding the intelligence, and one or two high-signal "surfaced for you" items.
**Why:** Establishes the surface and the position — Zavari as the calm coherence layer above a stack it doesn't own.
**Must achieve:** Instant orientation and trust; vendor-neutrality felt at a glance (no vendor dominates; first-party tools sit as peers); authoritative yet effortless to read.

### A2 — Strategic Opportunity Brief
**Shows:** The marquee output — a triangulated answer to a real strategic question (e.g., *"Should we build the enterprise tier?"*): coherence findings, second-order findings, and convergence/contradiction, each sourced and confidence-rated, leading to a recommendation.
**Why:** The product's value moment — McKinsey-grade synthesis, continuous, defensible, sourced.
**Must achieve:** Feels like something a senior leader would stake a decision on; traceability is native and reassuring; reads like a considered document, not a data dump.

### A3 — Coherence map *(marquee differentiator)*
**Shows:** The company's active initiatives and bets, with the north star as the anchor, surfacing where bets conflict or fail to ladder up.
**Why:** The screen no single-ecosystem incumbent could produce — coherence detection needs breadth across the whole vendor-neutral stack.
**Must achieve:** Misalignment is *obvious and intuitive* at a glance — clear, even a little reassuring, never alarming or busy. The clearest visual proof of the durable wedge.

### A4 — Strategy-to-execution / Decision Record
**Shows:** A delivery artifact (e.g., a Jira-style epic) carrying a provenance link back to its originating Brief and OKR — the unbroken chain from signal → strategy → delivery — and/or the auditable Decision Record (what was decided, why, on what signals, and whether the outcome validated it).
**Why:** Demonstrates the strategy-to-execution bridge and the institutional-memory moat.
**Must achieve:** The "why this exists" lineage feels effortless to follow; the chain feels unbroken and trustworthy.

### A5 — Two-pronged entry ("connect yours · use ours")
**Shows:** The onboarding/configuration surface that makes the two on-ramps explicit and **co-equal**: connect the existing stack (organized by signal category) *side-by-side* with adopting Zavari's own lightweight tools, with the framing "use our tools for less, and turn on the strategic intelligence when you're ready."
**Why:** The Phase 2 thesis requires the vendor-neutral, two-pronged position be visible. This is that screen — and it must show first-party tools as a genuine, equal path.
**Must achieve:** Both paths feel real and equally weighted; the cold-start solution and the structural reason an incumbent can't copy the position both come through; choosing "use ours" feels light and inviting.

## Group B — The First-party Point Solutions ("use ours")

*The lightweight tools a team can adopt directly — the alternative to BYO Jira / Amplitude / Dovetail / Condens. A representative workflow showing they are light, inviting, and feed the same intelligence layer. Cover research, roadmap, and planning; decide the exact screens.*

### B-intent — what this workflow must convey
A team that doesn't want to wire up (or pay for) a heavy incumbent stack can adopt Zavari's own tools and get value immediately — and that signal flows straight into the coherence layer. These screens should feel **noticeably lighter and friendlier** than the enterprise incumbents they replace, while clearly belonging to the same family as the intelligence layer. They are the concrete face of the "use ours" prong note 1 asked us to elevate.

**Representative surfaces to cover (merge/split as you see fit):**
- **Research** — a lightweight qualitative research tool (the Dovetail / Condens alternative): customer interview insights captured and themed, ready to feed the Triangulation Engine. Should feel effortless next to a heavyweight research platform.
- **Roadmap** — a lightweight roadmap tool: initiatives laddered to outcomes and the north star. The friendly alternative to a heavy planning suite.
- **Planning** — a lightweight planning/OKR tool: a cycle's objectives and priorities, drafted and managed simply.
- **The hand-off moment** — how signal from these first-party tools visibly flows into the Strategic Intelligence Layer ("turn on the intelligence"), making the two prongs feel like one system.

**Must achieve across Group B:** light, inviting, genuinely easy to adopt; unmistakably the same brand family as Group A; and a clear sense that using these tools *seeds the intelligence layer* — the point solutions and the strategic layer are one product, not two.

## Design anchors
- **Design system:** Zavari Design System (Claude Design Project 1) — established brand, IBM Plex (default), Navy/Cyan/Azure.
- **Implementation target:** Tailwind CSS (Phase 6) — keep structure Tailwind-friendly.
- **Primary user:** VP Product / CPO / Head of Strategy at a growth-stage product org. Desktop-first, high-focus.
- **Viewport:** Desktop.
- **Fidelity target:** High enough to evaluate the concept and feel like a real product — not pixel-final, not a wireframe.
- **Emotional north star:** authoritative *and* genuinely intuitive — effortless to use, never technical or cold.

---

## What these screens are NOT
Concept screens for a commit-or-kill gate decision, not production designs. No settings / account / auth plumbing, no need to be pixel-final or to represent a complete product. Design the product, not the plumbing.
