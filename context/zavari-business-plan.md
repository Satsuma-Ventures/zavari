## Executive Summary

Zavari is an AI-powered strategic intelligence platform purpose-built for product organizations at Series B–D companies. It functions as the operating system beneath a product organization's existing tool stack — ingesting signals from qualitative research, product analytics, market intelligence, CRM, documentation, and delivery management — and synthesizing them through its core mechanism, the Triangulation Engine, to produce validated strategic recommendations, cascading OKRs, roadmap artifacts, and unprompted market alerts.

The problem Zavari solves is structural, not technical. Product organizations are not short on tools. They are short on the intelligence layer that connects them. Every planning cycle, CPOs and VPs of Product manually synthesize what their qualitative research, behavioral analytics, and market signals say — across five or more disconnected platforms — into strategic decisions that determine the next quarter's priorities. That synthesis is ad hoc, undocumented, and unrepeatable. When the VP of Product leaves, the reasoning leaves with them.

Zavari makes that synthesis continuous, auditable, and institutional.

The market is large, underserved at the Series B–D tier, and in motion. The Microsoft Viva Goals shutdown (December 2025) displaced thousands of organizations from their OKR infrastructure. The Atlassian and Miro ecosystems are expanding aggressively but remain siloed within their own data. No established player combines multi-signal triangulation with product-strategy-specific outputs for the growth-stage product org buyer. The window to define this category is open and closing.

Zavari is seeking pre-seed capital to fund technical co-founder recruitment, design partner validation, and a Lovable POC that serves as both a prospect validation tool and a co-founder attraction vehicle.

## The Problem

Product leaders at Series B–D companies face a consistent, structural planning problem. They have:

- Qualitative research in Dovetail or Confluence — what customers say
- Behavioral analytics in Amplitude or Mixpanel — what customers do
- Competitive signals scattered across Crayon, G2, and ad hoc web searches — what the market is doing
- Customer feedback in CRM and sales call recordings — what prospects are asking for
- Strategy documentation in Notion or Google Docs — what leadership decided last quarter

None of these tools talk to each other. Every planning cycle, synthesis happens manually — in a CPO's head, a VP's slide deck, or a three-hour offsite with sticky notes. The result is decisions that are defensible in the meeting but untraceable afterward, based on whichever signals were loudest rather than which signals were most accurate, and disconnected from the execution that follows.

This is not a tool problem. Product organizations already have too many tools. It is a coordinating intelligence problem — the absence of a layer that sits above the stack, synthesizes across it, and produces defensible recommendations from the combination rather than from any single source.

The consequences are measurable: strategic decisions that are revisited every quarter because the reasoning was never documented, competitive moves that surface in sprint retrospectives rather than strategic planning, and CPO attrition that takes institutional memory with it because no system holds it.

## The Solution

Zavari is the operating system for strategic product decisions. It operates across three layers simultaneously:

**The operating layer** connects the tools product organizations already use — qualitative research platforms, analytics, competitive intelligence, roadmapping, delivery management, CRM, documentation — into a unified intelligence workflow.

**The system of record** captures not just what was decided, but the intelligence that drove each decision, the signals that were triangulated, and the outcomes that followed. Decision quality becomes auditable. Strategic learning compounds.

**The organizational intelligence layer** runs continuously — monitoring the signal sources that matter, surfacing what nobody thought to ask for, and alerting the team before the next planning cycle discovers a problem that has been visible in the data for weeks.

The mechanism that makes this work is the **Triangulation Engine** — Zavari's core differentiator. Named for the GPS analogy at the heart of the company's founding story (see Foundational Philosophy), the Triangulation Engine resolves across three independent signal types: first-party qualitative (what customers say), first-party quantitative (what customers do), and third-party market data (what the market is doing). Convergence across all three produces high-confidence strategic conclusions. Contradiction between them surfaces the strategic tension worth examining. Silence in one category flags a research gap and triggers a workflow to fill it.

The outputs: Strategic Opportunity Briefs, cascading OKR structures, roadmap artifacts that link to execution tools, and unprompted signal alerts when converging market conditions warrant attention.

## Porter's Five Forces

### Force 1: Threat of New Entrants — HIGH

The barriers to entry for AI-powered product intelligence tools are relatively low at the feature level. Any well-funded startup can build a point solution for qualitative research synthesis or competitive monitoring. The specific threat is from well-capitalized AI-native startups (e.g., ProductNow, $6M seed July 2025) positioning with similar language. Zavari's defensible barriers are: (1) the Triangulation Engine's cross-source synthesis architecture, which is harder to replicate than any individual integration; (2) the strategic learning layer, which compounds with each customer's decision history; (3) founder domain expertise that drives integration depth and product intuition that cannot be hired quickly.

*Strategic response:* Build integrations and accumulate strategic learning data faster than new entrants can catch up. First-mover advantage in category definition and customer strategic records is Zavari's primary early moat.

### Force 2: Threat of Substitutes — HIGH

The most significant substitute is not a competing product but the status quo: a skilled CPO manually synthesizing signals from existing tools. Every Series B–D company already has a planning process; the question is whether Zavari's output is sufficiently better to justify change management and budget allocation. Secondary substitutes are the "good enough AI" tools already in place — Atlassian Rovo, Microsoft Copilot, Notion AI, Glean — which reduce perceived switching costs by making buyers feel they already have intelligence capabilities.

*Strategic response:* Zavari must demonstrate that triangulated strategic intelligence produces materially better decisions than single-source synthesis or general-purpose AI. Early design partner proof points showing specific revenue-attributable decisions are the primary weapon against the substitution objection.

### Force 3: Bargaining Power of Buyers — MODERATE

At the Series B–D stage, buyers (CPOs and VPs of Product) have moderate bargaining power. They have multiple tools competing for their attention and budget. They are also sophisticated buyers who will demand proof before committing. However, the specific pain Zavari addresses — manual synthesis across disconnected tools — is both acute and visible to these buyers, reducing their inclination to negotiate on price if the value proposition is demonstrated. As the strategic learning layer compounds, switching costs increase and buyer power decreases over time.

*Strategic response:* Founder-led sales with high-touch design partner pricing ($20K–$40K/year) builds the proof points necessary to shift buyer leverage. Outcome-based pricing exploration should begin in Year 2 to align Zavari's incentives with buyer results.

### Force 4: Bargaining Power of Suppliers — LOW TO MODERATE

Zavari's primary supplier dependency is Anthropic (Claude Sonnet and Opus for the AI layer). This creates meaningful supplier concentration risk — if Anthropic changes pricing, API terms, or model availability, it materially affects Zavari's cost structure and capability. Secondary dependencies include CrewAI for agent orchestration and the integration platforms (Amplitude, Dovetail, Jira, etc.) whose APIs Zavari depends on.

*Strategic response:* Monitor Anthropic pricing trajectory and maintain model abstraction in the architecture to allow substitution if necessary. Build integration connectors against category-level APIs (not tool-specific SDKs where avoidable) to reduce lock-in to any single vendor's API.

### Force 5: Competitive Rivalry — HIGH AND INTENSIFYING

Rivalry is high because the category is being entered from multiple directions simultaneously. Productboard and Dovetail are converging from product management. Amplitude and Mixpanel are converging from analytics. Atlassian is expanding via Rovo and Jira Product Discovery. Miro is entering via Product Acceleration. WorkBoard owns strategy execution. None of them has reached Zavari's cross-source synthesis position — but the convergence pace is accelerating, with multiple companies shipping directly relevant features in 2025–2026.

*Strategic response:* Speed matters more than perfection at this stage. The window to establish category leadership is 12–18 months. Zavari must reach design partner validation and early commercial customers before ecosystem convergence closes the differentiation gap.

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths

**Founder domain expertise.** Matt brings direct practitioner credibility with senior product leadership roles at Expedia Group, Virtuoso Travel, and Porch Group — precisely the ICP Zavari serves. The Triangulation Engine was not conceived from market research; it was derived from lived strategic planning experience and validated by analogy with physical GPS triangulation. This gives Zavari a product intuition and customer empathy advantage that cannot be quickly replicated.

**Architectural differentiation.** The cross-source Triangulation Engine — resolving across first-party qualitative, first-party quantitative, and third-party market intelligence — is genuinely novel in the product intelligence market. No current competitor synthesizes across all three signal types simultaneously.

**Compounding moat.** The strategic learning layer creates a compounding competitive advantage that improves with each customer's usage. Decision history, signal reliability calibration, and strategic vocabulary accumulation make Zavari progressively harder to displace — the opposite of commodity AI tools.

**Clear product vision.** The Foundational Philosophy, Solution Architecture, and Competitive Landscape are documented, aligned, and defensible. This gives Zavari an unusually strong epistemic foundation for a pre-seed company, which matters for co-founder recruitment and investor confidence.

**Market timing.** The Viva Goals shutdown, Institutional AI recognition (a16z, March 2026), and accelerating enterprise AI adoption create a receptive market precisely when Zavari is entering.

### Weaknesses

**No technical co-founder yet.** The most significant execution risk at this stage. The Triangulation Engine and integration architecture require strong engineering leadership that cannot be bootstrapped from Lovable. Co-founder recruitment is the top priority.

**No revenue or design partners.** Zavari is pre-revenue with no customer proof points. The product thesis is grounded in founder experience and market research, but buyer validation has not yet occurred.

**Single founder until co-founder joins.** Investor committees and potential partners will scrutinize the single-founder risk until the team is formed.

**Integration build cost.** The BYO processes commitment and eight-category integration model is the right architectural bet but requires significant engineering investment to execute. Prioritization and phasing must be disciplined.

**Unsourced legacy data.** Some figures from early project knowledge documents (the 34-week cycle metric, the 70% strategic initiative failure rate) were generated without credible citations and have been removed from current materials. Any future claims require verified sourcing.

### Opportunities

**Viva Goals displacement.** Thousands of organizations lost their native OKR platform on December 31, 2025 with no Microsoft replacement. This is an active, time-sensitive market opening for a product that combines strategic intelligence with OKR management.

**Institutional AI category emergence.** The a16z framework for Institutional vs. Individual AI (March 2026) is creating category awareness among investors and enterprise buyers. Zavari is well-positioned to lead this category definition in the product organization context.

**Ecosystem partner network.** Atlassian Marketplace, Notion's integration directory, and Linear's partner ecosystem represent distribution channels that could accelerate customer acquisition without proportional sales cost.

**White space at Series B–D.** Enterprise intelligence platforms (Palantir, WorkBoard, AlphaSense) are priced and architected for large enterprises. Series B–D product orgs have acute need and available budget but no purpose-built solution at accessible price points.

**Technical co-founder as strategic multiplier.** The right technical co-founder brings engineering execution, investor credibility, and potential network effects in the Series B–D product engineering community that Zavari targets.

**Ecosystem convergence.** Atlassian (Rovo + JPD + acquisitions), Miro (Product Acceleration), Amplitude (Agentic AI Analytics + Kraftful), and Dovetail (AI Agents + Docs) are all shipping features that approach Zavari's value proposition from adjacent positions. The 12–18 month window is real and closing.

**AI infrastructure cost risk.** Claude API pricing at the Opus model level (required for Triangulation Engine reasoning quality) creates significant cost-of-goods pressure at scale. If Anthropic pricing changes unfavorably, margins compress.

**Fundraising environment.** Pre-seed AI startups face increasing investor scrutiny. The wave of AI tool investments from 2023–2024 has produced mixed results, making investors more selective about differentiation and defensibility.

## PESTLE Analysis

### Political

**Enterprise AI regulation.** The EU AI Act (in force 2024–2025) and emerging US AI governance frameworks affect how Zavari can process and store customer data, particularly qualitative research and CRM data that may contain personal information. GDPR compliance is non-negotiable for European customers.

**Data sovereignty.** Growing requirements for data residency (particularly in Germany, France, and regulated industries) will require consideration in infrastructure architecture. WorkBoard's European competitor Workpath has gained ground specifically by offering EU data residency.

**US-China technology tensions.** For customers with global operations, sourcing AI infrastructure from US providers (Anthropic, AWS, Google) may trigger procurement scrutiny in certain markets.

*Implication:* Privacy-by-design architecture from day one. Consider EU data residency as a mid-term infrastructure requirement. Not a blocker at seed stage, but a deployment constraint to plan around.

### Economic

**Series B–D market health.** Zavari's ICP is growth-stage companies — a segment sensitive to venture funding cycles. A contraction in Series B–D funding (as occurred in 2022–2023) reduces the number of qualifying prospects and their willingness to invest in new tooling. The 2025–2026 funding environment has improved from the 2022–2023 lows but remains disciplined.

**Enterprise AI spending growth.** Menlo Ventures' 2025 State of Generative AI report estimates enterprise AI spending growing at 30%+ CAGR. This creates favorable budget headwinds for Zavari as product organizations seek to demonstrate AI ROI.

**Tool consolidation pressure.** Series B–D companies are actively rationalizing SaaS spend. This creates the "fewer tools with more intelligence" buyer disposition that benefits a consolidating intelligence layer — but also means Zavari needs to displace existing budget rather than capture new budget.

*Implication:* Position Zavari as a consolidator, not an addition. Calculate ROI on tools it replaces, not just time it saves.

### Social

**Product leader mobility.** VPs of Product and CPOs change roles frequently. This creates both a risk (customer champion attrition) and an opportunity (a champion who had Zavari at one company becomes a buyer at the next). The strategic learning layer mitigates the risk by institutionalizing intelligence independent of individual tenure.

**Remote and distributed product teams.** The shift to distributed work has increased the coordination cost of strategic planning — planning ceremonies have become harder to run well, which increases the value of asynchronous intelligence tools.

**AI fatigue vs. AI urgency.** Product leaders simultaneously experience AI feature overload and genuine urgency to find AI tools that produce real outcomes. Zavari must cut through noise by demonstrating institutional-grade intelligence rather than another AI feature.

*Implication:* The "decisions-to-delivery cycles measured in quarters, not weeks" framing resonates with distributed product teams who feel the coordination cost acutely.

### Technological

**Foundation model rapid improvement.** Claude, GPT, and Gemini capabilities are improving faster than the industry can absorb. This is a tailwind for Zavari's Triangulation Engine quality — better reasoning models produce better strategic synthesis — but also means today's differentiating AI capability may be commoditized in 12–24 months.

**Multi-agent framework maturity.** CrewAI and similar multi-agent orchestration frameworks are maturing rapidly, making Zavari's agent architecture more accessible to build and less proprietary as a technical moat. The moat shifts from the architecture itself to the integration depth and strategic learning layer.

**MCP (Model Context Protocol).** Anthropic's MCP standard is emerging as a connector protocol for AI tools. Multiple platforms (Klue, Linear, Atlassian, Amplitude) have shipped MCP servers in 2025, which will reduce Zavari's integration build cost for those platforms.

**Data warehouse as universal integration layer.** Snowflake, BigQuery, and Databricks are increasingly the canonical data source for organizational analytics. Integrating with the data warehouse rather than individual analytics tools is a more durable architectural bet for quantitative signal ingestion.

*Implication:* Build on MCP where available; prioritize data warehouse integration alongside point-tool connectors; treat AI model quality as a floor that keeps rising rather than a fixed capability.

### Legal

**Intellectual property.** AI-generated strategic recommendations may create IP questions — if Zavari synthesizes customer data to produce a competitive insight, who owns that insight? These questions are evolving rapidly in the legal and product communities. Zavari's terms of service must address data ownership clearly.

**Customer data security.** Strategic briefs and decision records contain some of the most sensitive organizational information — competitive strategy, market positioning, product bets. Enterprise customers will require SOC 2 Type II compliance before deployment. This is a Year 2 investment but should be architected for from day one.

**Employment law.** As an AI-powered system influencing strategic decisions and OKRs that affect headcount, resource allocation, and organizational structure, Zavari could be cited as influencing employment decisions in jurisdictions with AI-in-employment regulations.

*Implication:* Engage legal counsel early on data processing agreements and AI decision attribution language. SOC 2 compliance on the product roadmap for Year 2.

### Environmental

**AI compute carbon footprint.** Large language model inference at the scale Zavari requires (Claude Opus for Triangulation Engine) has meaningful energy consumption. As enterprise customers increasingly scrutinize vendor sustainability, Zavari may face ESG-related procurement questions.

**Remote-first company structure.** Zavari's bootstrapped-then-funded model can be fully distributed from inception, which reduces the physical footprint and infrastructure cost compared to traditional SaaS companies.

*Implication:* Anthropic's carbon offset commitments and cloud infrastructure efficiency are partial mitigations. ESG reporting will be a procurement question at larger enterprise customers.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Design Partner Program (Months 1–9)

The design partner program is Zavari's primary go-to-market motion at seed stage. It serves three functions simultaneously: product validation, revenue seed, and proof point generation.

**Target:** 5–10 design partners who are VPs of Product or CPOs at Series B–D technology companies, with active product organizations and demonstrable planning pain. Design partners must be accessible (founder can meet with them regularly), willing to share strategic context with the product, and credible as references.

**Sourcing:** Founder's direct network (Expedia, Virtuoso, Porch, UT Austin McCombs alumni, LinkedIn thought leadership community). Secondary: warm introductions from angel investors and advisors.

**Offer:** Heavily discounted or partially deferred pricing ($10K–$20K/year vs. eventual $50K+ commercial price) in exchange for structured access — monthly working sessions, willingness to be quoted, and shared outcome data.

**Success criteria:** At least 5 design partners completing a full planning cycle using Zavari, with at least 3 willing to speak to a specific strategic decision Zavari influenced and its outcome.

### Phase 2: Early Commercial (Months 9–18)

With proof points from the design partner program, Zavari moves to founder-led sales targeting a first cohort of 20–40 paying customers at $40,000–$60,000 ACV.

**Channels:**
- LinkedIn thought leadership — the most consistent channel for reaching CPOs and VPs of Product at target companies. Matt's existing content strategy continues and expands with Zavari case study content
- Integration marketplace presence — listed in Atlassian Marketplace, Notion's integrations directory, and Linear's partner ecosystem
- Product community seeding — Lenny's Newsletter partnerships, Product-Led Alliance, SVPG community sponsorship
- Warm referral program — design partners introduce Zavari to peers at peer-stage companies

**Sales motion:** Founder-led, with CPO/VP Product as primary buyer and champion. The "Zavari Strategic Intelligence Review" — a 45-minute session where Matt uses Zavari's framework to analyze a prospect's current planning process — as the primary proof-of-concept meeting.

**Pricing model at commercial launch — platform + seat:**

Zavari uses a platform fee structure rather than pure per-seat pricing. This is intentional: it prevents direct unit economics comparison with per-seat tools like Productboard ($59/maker/month) or Coworker.ai, and better reflects where Zavari's value actually lives — the Triangulation Engine and integration infrastructure, not headcount.

| Component | Price | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Platform fee (annual) | $18,000–$24,000/year | Covers Triangulation Engine, integration layer, market intelligence synthesis, strategic learning infrastructure |
| Creator seats | $150–$200/seat/month (annual) | Command layer users: CPO, VP Product, senior PMs who open strategic questions and accept briefs |
| Contributor access | Included | Engineering leads, executives, and delivery teams who view OKR structures and execution artifacts in their native tools |

- Creator seats (4 × $175/month × 12): $8,400/year

**Illustrative ACV for a 20-person product org (6 Creator seats):**

- Creator seats (6 × $175/month × 12): $12,600/year

Target ACV range: $25,000–$50,000, scaling with org size. Minimum engagement: $20,000/year.

**Medium-term pricing evolution — outcome-based:**
From Year 2, explore outcome-based pricing tiers that supplement the platform fee — for example, a percentage of strategic initiative value confirmed through Zavari's outcome tracking layer. This aligns Zavari's incentives directly with customer results and is the natural evolution of the revenue outcomes principle in the Foundational Philosophy.

With $1M–$2.4M ARR, Zavari pursues:
- First non-founder sales hire (VP of Sales or first Account Executive from product tools background)
