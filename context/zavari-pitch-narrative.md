## The Hook

The tools exist. What's missing is the OS that makes them work as one.

Every product organization at a Series B–D company is running the same stack: Dovetail for customer research, Amplitude for usage data, Crayon for competitive intelligence, Gong for sales signals, Notion for documentation. Individually, each tool is good at what it does. Together, they produce nothing. The signals sit in silos. Nobody synthesizes them. And every quarter, a CPO or VP of Product spends a week doing it manually before the next planning cycle begins.

That's not strategy. That's information routing. And it's the problem Zavari solves.

## The Moment We're In

AI made every individual 10× more productive. No company became 10× more valuable.

That gap — between individual AI productivity and organizational AI value — is the defining product challenge of 2026. George Sivulka (a16z) framed it precisely: the gains are real at the individual level and invisible at the institutional level.

Freda Duan (Altimeter Capital) explains why. Organizational hierarchy exists primarily to solve an information routing problem. The org is too large for any single person to see the whole picture, so you install layers of managers to aggregate signals, synthesize them, and pass strategic intent back down. Most of what we call "strategic planning" is actually expensive translation work — PM intent encoded into a document, a designer decoding and re-encoding it, an engineer doing the same. Every translation loses fidelity. Every translation generates wait time. Every translation requires alignment meetings.

AI collapses this. But only if you build the infrastructure for it. Most companies are using AI the way you'd use a faster horse — accelerating individual tasks without rethinking the workflow. The companies that pull ahead will be the ones whose organizational intelligence compounds over time. That's not execution speed. It's learning speed.

Zavari is the infrastructure for learning speed.

The scale of the problem is confirmed by competitors' own research. Miro commissioned Forrester and Harvard Business Review Analytic Services to survey 850 product, IT, and line-of-business leaders in September 2025. The findings: 87% say highly effective product development is critical to business success — but only 34% describe their organization as achieving it. 76% of product leaders say most AI tools target individual, not team, productivity, and rank this as their top blocker to AI ROI. Only 10% say AI in product development is primarily focused on helping teams collaborate.

The individual-to-institutional gap is validated at scale, by a competitor's budget.

## The Problem

A typical product organization at Series B–D is making strategic decisions from five or more disconnected signal sources. Nobody has connected them. The synthesis happens manually, in one person's head, during the two weeks before each planning cycle. The reasoning is never documented. When that person leaves, it leaves with them.

The consequences are structural, not operational:

**Decisions-to-delivery** are measured in quarters, not weeks — not because teams are slow, but because the translation chain from strategic intent to execution artifact is long and lossy.

**Institutional memory** doesn't exist. Every VP of Product who joins inherits a partial picture. Every planning cycle partially re-litigates decisions already made.

**Translation cost** is the hidden overhead. The CPO synthesizing five tools manually isn't doing strategic work — they're doing information routing. Every handoff from signal to recommendation degrades fidelity.

**Strategic analysis** at the quality level that matters requires either a $50K–$500K consulting engagement for a point-in-time analysis, or a very experienced CPO with the bandwidth to do it continuously. Most Series B–D companies have neither.

The choice has been: fly blind, synthesize manually, or pay a consultant. Zavari is the fourth option.

## The Solution

Zavari is the operating system for strategic product decisions.

The OS framing is precise, not marketing language. An OS doesn't replace the applications running on top of it — it coordinates them. Zavari doesn't ask product teams to replace Dovetail, Amplitude, or Jira. It runs beneath them, connects them, and produces the intelligence layer those tools individually cannot.

Zavari's architecture is organized around six named capabilities:

**Triangulation Engine** — the cross-source synthesis core. Ingests first-party qualitative signals (what customers say), first-party quantitative signals (what customers do), and third-party market signals (what the market is doing), and resolves across all three to produce Strategic Opportunity Briefs. Where signals converge: high-confidence strategy. Where they conflict: the tension worth examining. The framework was developed during an MBA internship at Dell (End User Computing strategy) under Ansgar Chorhummel, and confirmed at Magellan GPS.

**Strategic Instinct** — the proactive signal surfacing capability. Every competing platform is query-driven: you must ask it a question. If you don't know to ask, you don't get the answer. Strategic Instinct monitors continuously between planning cycles, watching signal patterns across all connected sources. When something matters — a competitive move that conflicts with a current strategic bet, a customer sentiment shift that contradicts a product assumption — Zavari surfaces it before the next offsite discovers it. The team didn't ask for it. Zavari surfaced it because the signals said something mattered. This develops through the Strategic Learning Layer: the sharper the system's understanding of what matters to this organization, the sharper its instinct for what to surface.

**Strategic Learning Layer** — the institutional memory. Every Strategic Opportunity Brief, every accepted OKR, every outcome tracking record is versioned and stored. Signal reliability calibrates against actual outcomes. When someone new joins, they inherit a structured record of why the company is where it is. Strategic quality compounds over time and survives personnel changes.

**Execution Bridge** — the strategy-to-delivery translation layer. Accepted strategic recommendations don't stop at the Brief. The Execution Bridge translates them into OKR structures, initiative definitions, and epic-level execution artifacts — with the strategic reasoning embedded at every level. The chain from signal to strategy to delivery is unbroken and traceable.

**Decision Record** — the auditable reasoning chain from signal to recommendation to decision to outcome. A CPO should be able to show not just what was decided, but why, what signals supported it, and whether the outcome validated the reasoning. As AI decision-making faces increasing governance scrutiny, this becomes a compliance asset as well as a strategic one.

**Signal Architecture** — the integration design and category model. Zavari integrates with tools by category, not by specific vendor: qualitative research platforms, quantitative analytics platforms, market and industry intelligence, product planning tools, project and delivery management, documentation and knowledge management, CRM and sales signal platforms, and data warehouse. A company that switches from Dovetail to Condens reconfigures its Signal Architecture without rebuilding its intelligence workflows. This is a north star commitment: the moat grows as signal categories expand, and switching costs compound with each additional integration.

These six capabilities are not independently purchasable products. They are architectural layers of a single integrated system. Each one compounds the others.

## Two Surfaces

Zavari's intelligence reaches the organization through two surfaces.

The **Strategic Command Layer** is the primary interface for CPOs, VPs of Product, and Heads of Strategy. This is where strategic questions are opened, Opportunity Briefs are reviewed and accepted, the quarterly OKR structure is drafted and managed, outcome health is monitored against commitments, and Strategic Instinct alerts are triaged. It's designed for the 3–6 people in an organization whose primary job is thinking about what to build and why.

**Execution Surfaces** reach the broader team through the tools they already use. Strategic context embedded in Jira epics. Accepted Briefs and OKR structures written to Confluence as the system of record. Strategic Instinct alerts surfaced in Slack before the team would have discovered them in a meeting. Read-only and context-providing — not input-capturing — because delivery teams shouldn't have to think about the intelligence layer to benefit from it.

Same intelligence layer, different surfaces.
